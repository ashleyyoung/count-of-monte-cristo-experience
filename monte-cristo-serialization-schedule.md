# The Count of Monte Cristo — Original Serialization Schedule

*Le Comte de Monte-Cristo* by Alexandre Dumas (père) as it originally appeared
in the Paris newspaper **Journal des débats politiques et littéraires**,
**28 August 1844 – 16 January 1846**.

It ran in **4 installment-runs ("parts")** totaling **139 separate appearances** —
usually a chapter or half-chapter per issue, frequently cut off mid-scene. Note the
**seven-month hiatus** between Part 2 (ends 26 Nov 1844) and Part 3 (resumes 20 Jun 1845):
the original audience genuinely waited that long.

*(Installment dates traced from the Bibliothèque nationale de France's Gallica archive.
Chapter titles follow the common English translation; "(cont.)" marks a chapter continued
from the previous installment.)*

---

## How a book group might use this

A few options, from most to least faithful:

- **Purist (4 meetings):** one meeting per newspaper part. Optional: leave a real gap
  (a few weeks) after Part 2 to honor the hiatus.
- **Weekly feuilleton feel:** read ~3–5 installments per week and meet monthly. The cliffhanger
  rhythm is the whole point — try stopping exactly where an installment stopped.
- **Hardcore:** read strictly by the dates below, ideally pausing where each issue paused.
